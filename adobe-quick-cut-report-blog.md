# Adobe Just Launched an AI Tool That Edits Your Videos For You (Sort Of)

*February 26, 2026 | 4 min read*

---

Adobe dropped something new this week, and if you've ever opened a video project, stared at 3 hours of raw footage, and quietly questioned your life choices — this one's for you.

The company launched **Quick Cut** in beta on February 25, a new AI-powered feature inside Adobe Firefly's video editor that takes your raw footage and assembles a structured first cut automatically. You describe what you're going for, it does the heavy lifting, and somewhere in the middle of that transaction, a rough edit appears where a blank timeline used to be.

---

## Here's How It Actually Works

Users upload their b-roll footage — or generate new AI clips directly inside Firefly — then describe the kind of video they're making. Interview, product demo, travel vlog, unboxing. Quick Cut analyzes the footage, identifies key moments, removes filler, and assembles a narrative-first rough cut.

From there, editors can select aspect ratios, set pacing preferences, choose a specific duration, and even feed the tool a shot list or script for more precision. The results come back as a multi-track edit inside Firefly's interface, ready to be refined.

Adobe's head of product marketing, Mike Polner, described it as going from *"I have clips"* to *"I have an edit I can work with"* — and honestly, that's a pretty accurate way to put it.

---

## What Adobe Is Saying About It

The company is pitching Quick Cut as a tool that removes the manual labor of assembly — not the creative decisions that come after. Senior director of product management Mike Folgner told The Verge that some parts of video editing "really are tedious" and that the goal is to "remove tedium and give you something to react to, but give you full control from there."

Adobe demonstrated the feature using footage from a gaming console controller review. The resulting edit needed refinement — something Adobe openly acknowledged — but the speed was notable. What would take a human editor considerable time to rough-cut manually, Quick Cut assembled in seconds.

The company is positioning this as a creative accelerant for content creators, marketers, and journalists who need rapid turnaround — not a replacement for the editing process itself.

---

## Where It Fits in Adobe's Bigger Strategy

Quick Cut arrives as Adobe continues expanding Firefly's video capabilities in what's becoming a competitive race in AI-generated media. OpenAI's Sora and Google DeepMind's Veo are both pushing into similar territory, and Adobe is making its moves accordingly.

One distinction Adobe consistently highlights: Firefly models were trained exclusively on licensed Adobe Stock content and public domain material — a deliberate choice that provides some legal insulation as copyright lawsuits continue to target other AI companies over training data practices. This matters particularly in a post-WGA and SAG-AFTRA strike landscape where Hollywood and creative industries are still actively negotiating the terms of AI usage.

Adobe also announced that new Firefly Pro and Premium subscribers who sign up before March 16 will receive unlimited image and video generations up to 2K resolution as part of a promotional offer.

---

## What's Coming Next

According to internal roadmaps shared with partners, Adobe plans to integrate Quick Cut more deeply into Premiere Pro later this year, alongside expanded Firefly Video Model capabilities. For professionals who live inside Premiere, that's the integration worth watching.

For now, Quick Cut is available in beta inside Firefly's video editor — a first step toward what Adobe is calling a "creative co-pilot" approach: AI handling the parts of production that are more mechanical, leaving the judgment calls to the humans in the room.

Whether that balance holds up in practice is something creators will figure out over the coming months. But as starting points go, an AI that at least hands you *something* to react to? There are worse places to begin.

---

*Quick Cut is currently available in beta for Adobe Firefly users.*
